The ferry docks at 09:30. The first thing we do is find the dunes — Zuid-Kennemerland National Park, grass and sand and the North Sea right there. Echo needs this. Honestly, we do too. The continent smells different before breakfast.

Day Two · Haarlem, Netherlands

Haarlem.
What Amsterdam
thinks it is.

Narrow brick streets. The Grote Markt. Boutiques in crooked old buildings. Jopenkerk — a gothic church turned brewery, which is exactly as good as it sounds. Dog-friendly terraces everywhere. Frites in a cone at dusk. Sleep in a bed that isn't moving.

Day Three · Haarlem

A Whole Day
With Nowhere
To Be

Frans Hals Museum if we're in the mood — 17th-century portraiture in a converted almshouse. Or not. A morning walk along the Spaarne. A terrace that earns its place. An Indonesian rijsttafel dinner, because the Dutch colonial pantry is extraordinary and we should use it. Whatever we don't do, we didn't need to.

Day Four · Gouda → Ghent

Cheese Stop.
Then Belgium
Announces Itself.

Gouda sits 45 minutes south. The cheese market has been weighing wheels in the same square since 1668. Aged Gouda has crystals in it like mineral deposits — it is a different substance dressed in a familiar name. We buy more than we think we need. Then southwest into Belgium, where Ghent's three towers appear on the horizon long before we arrive.

Gouda market Aged cheese with crystals Ghent · three towers
Day Five · Ghent, Belgium

Ghent is not
Bruges. Ghent
is a city.

The Graslei guild houses at dawn before the groups arrive. The Van Eyck altarpiece — the Adoration of the Mystic Lamb — fully restored and genuinely astonishing. Trappist ales and sour lambics. Waterzooi stew. The towers illuminated at evening, visible from somewhere we've chosen to be with that in mind.

Graslei waterfront Van Eyck altarpiece Trappist ales Patershol cobbles
Day Six · Le Touquet-Paris-Plage, France

A Beach We
Visited Once,
Eighteen Years Ago.

Twelve kilometres of sand. Pine forest dunes. Art Deco villas. Echo's first time in France, entirely unimpressed by the symbolism and deeply interested in the smells. The water is 14°C. We've both swum colder. Fish for dinner from Étaples up the river. Rest. This day deserves it.

12km beach Pine dunes 18 years returning Étaples fish
Day Seven · Calais → Folkestone → Walton-on-Trent

Hypermarket.
Tunnel.
Pete's Mum.

Cité Europe — an enormous temple of continental produce at the tunnel terminal. Wine, cheese, mustard, the particular biscuits. Forty-five minutes maximum. Then the Eurotunnel swallows the car and thirty-five minutes later deposits us in England. North to Walton-on-Trent. Share the cheese. Tell the stories.

Day Eight · South Acre, East Ayrshire

The Familiar
Turning.
Home.

North through Scotland. The light changes — that specific Scottish diffusion, the air that's more of itself. Nettle and Bramble, unimpressed. The chickens and ducks entirely indifferent. Echo finds her spot. Kettle on. The remaining wine opened. Outside: the particular green of home that isn't available anywhere else in Europe, because it's this one.
