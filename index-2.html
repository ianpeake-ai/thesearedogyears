Dog Years: An Invitation to the Long Story

Before there were kings or saints or cities, there was a fire in the dark.

And just beyond it, watching us, a shape with patient eyes.

One night — no one can say where — a child and a not-quite-wolf looked at one another across the glow and did not turn away. Something began there. It has been beginning ever since.

This space is about that.

Dog Years is a long, cross-time story about the oldest partnership we have: human and dog, side by side. Thirty-five thousand years of warmth, work, grief, fur, meat, loyalty, and the strange shared dream of staying alive together.

I'm not here to sentimentalise it.

I want to re-wild it.
I want to return its teeth.
I want to let it be strange again.

Dogs have been gods, workers, omens, status symbols, comrades, scapegoats, and family. They've slept in our beds and died in our wars. They've guarded our babies and eaten our dead. They've survived because of us and in spite of us — and we have done the same.

This is a project about that ancient contract:
the one we never signed, but kept anyway.

* * *

This isn't nostalgia. It's ancestry.

If you've ever looked into a dog's eyes and felt that jolt of recognition — that ancient, unreasonable love — you've met the thing I'm trying to write towards.

Dog Years is for the people who feel that without needing it explained.

The fire is small on purpose.
There's room.
Pull up a place by the flames.
